Magic for Beginners

Information for the new MTG Player. Learn about the formats and the decks for each them.

Standard

The Standard format uses the most recently released Magic sets. The following card sets are currently permitted in the Standard format:
  • Battle for Zendikar
  • Magic Origins
  • Dragons of Tarkir
  • Fate Reforged
  • Khans of Tarkir
Standard is also a constructed deck format, so the normal constructed rules apply:
  • Minimum of sixty cards
  • No maximum deck size, as long as you can shuffle your deck unassisted
  • Up to fifteen cards in your sideboard, if used
  • No more than four of any individual card in your main deck and sideboard combined (with the exception of basic lands)

Tier 1

The best of the best, these are the decks with some of the best tournament postings in recent months.

Dark Jeskai

Info

If you want to play the best cards available in Standard, go no farther. Great creatures and great spells equals a great deck

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Can take over a gmae quite quickly due to the card draw and removal

Weaknesses

  • Very expensive to build
  • It's only weakness is against itself, which is important to know as this is one of the most popular decks.
Atarka Red

Info

A mono-red aggro deck with a hint of combo. With its ability to overwhelm a game fast and win out of nowhere this a good deck for beginners.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Cheap creatures can overwhelm your opponent quickly.
  • Easy to learn.
  • Cheapest Tier 1 deck.

Weaknesses

  • If your creatures can't stay on board, you have a very difficult time ahead of you.
  • People come prepared for this match-up due to how common it is.
Abzan

Info

A very resilient three-coloured deck that time and again, wins tournaments it shouldn't.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Has little, to no, bad match-ups.
  • The longer a game goes the more likely you are to win.

Weaknesses

  • A bit pricy.
  • More skill intensive.

Tier 2

Might not be the best, but they do give Tier 1 a run for their money. These decks have good results and have good game against most of the Tier 1 decks.

Eldrazi Ramp

Info

If you like to play big creatures, then go no farther, this is the deck for you.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Has one of the best late-game in Standard.
  • Simple.

Weaknesses

  • Hard to beat aggro and other ramp decks.
Esper Dragons

Info

A Control deck geared towards aggressively controlling the game into a state where it can resolve a Dragonlord Ojutai to attempt to soft lock the opponent out of the game for the 4 turns in which the deck attacks with Dragonlord Ojutai.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Can beat any deck with a nut-draw.
  • Powerful creatures that are hard to get rid of.
  • Can win fast once its gets going

Weaknesses

  • Very expensive.
  • Most challenging deck to pilot.
  • Questionable match-ups against aggro.
4-Color Rally

Info

A graveyard based combo deck that wants to blow out opponents in one decisive turn.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Can win out of nowhere.
  • Hard to sideboard against.

Weaknesses

  • Skill intensive.
  • Usually needs Rally the Ancestors to win.
  • One card, Anafenza, the Foremost, shuts the deck down.
  • Slow.
