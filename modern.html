Magic for Beginners

Information for the new MTG Player. Learn about the formats and the decks for each them.

Modern

Modern is a constructed format and therefore adheres to the following constructed rules:

  • Minimum of sixty cards.
  • No maximum deck size, as long as you can shuffle your deck unassisted.
  • Up to fifteen cards in your sideboard, if used.
With the exception of basic lands (Plains, Island, Swamp, Mountain, and Forest), your combined deck and sideboard may not contain more than four of any individual card, counted by its English card title equivalent.

Tier 1

The best of the best, these are the decks with some of the best tournament postings in recent months.

Green/Red Tron

Info

Tron is a (mainly) green deck that's goal is to assemble the Urzatron (Urza's Tower, Urza's Mine, and Urza's Power Plant) as quickly as possible. Once they have been assembled, the goal is to generate insurmountable advantage. The main route to this is Karn Liberated, who is the cornerstone to the deck (yes, just as much as the Tron lands themselves). Once Karn takes the field, it becomes difficult or impossible for opponents to retake the game. A T3 Karn is this deck's goal, and often seals the game on the spot. Other than Karn, this deck has perhaps the best inevitability in the format. When you're done assembling the Urzatron with your land search, you can search out Eye of Ugin which in turn searches out Emrakul, the Aeons Torn. Generally castable between turns 6 and 8, this single card makes sure that your opponents can never even hope to stall you out.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Gx Tron is fast, consistent, and has one of the best lategame in all of Modern.
  • It has generally positive matchups against most decks.
  • It's a relatively simple deck to pick up, while still holding a lot of intricacies in terms of its card choices

Weaknesses

  • Has a hard time against fast decks such as Burn and Infect, which are relatively common.
  • If your opponent casts a Blood Moon, it is virtually unwinnable.
Burn

Info

Burn is a deck that uses efficient creatures and Red instant and sorcery spells to deal direct damage to your opponent in an effort to end the game as quickly as possible using little to no disruption

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Incredibly fast, sometimes able to win on turn 3, usually turn 4.
  • Relatively cheap when compared to other decks.
  • Very easy to learn.
  • It does not have an unwinnable matchup.

Weaknesses

  • You have a set amount of damage you can deal per game, if your oppnent gains some life, which is very common, you may be unable to win.
Affinity

Info

One of the few real aggro deck in the format with elements of combo and tempo. It combines low-costed creatures with evasion and highly efficient spells in the form of Thoughtcast and Galvanic Blast, and it is also highly resilient to removal and board wipes. The deck has numerous complex synergies that rewards strong pilots with faster clocks. Theoretically, the deck can win on as early as turn 2, although in reality it mostly wins on turn 4 or turn 5 with light disruption.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Can win on turn 2, usually turn 3/4.
  • Can win through multiple means.
  • Pretty good matchups on the whole.

Weaknesses

  • Game 2 and 3, opponents can bring in cards that make winning quite difficult.
  • Requires more skill than expected.
  • Can run out of gas quite fast.
Twin

Info

A combo deck that, more often then not, wins on turn 4.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Wins consistently on Turn 4.
  • If the combo doesn't work can win through other means.
  • Not many weaknesses.
  • Many different variants.

Weaknesses

  • Sometimes you can't find a wincon.
  • Many decks are now running more removal to deal with the combo.

Tier 2

Might not be the best, but they do give Tier 1 a run for their money. These decks have good results and have good game against most of the Tier 1 decks.

Collected Elves

Info

An aggro-based deck that wins off the speed in which it assembles an army of murderous elves.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Fast and consistent.
  • Cheap

Weaknesses

  • Occassionaly, even if things come together you can still lose.
  • Board wipes are very effective against you.
Merfolk

Info

Merfolk is a blue-based aggro deck with tempo elements that is built around the synergistic elements available to the Merfolk creature type. Merfolk is a mostly fair, highly linear, proactive deck that employs lots of cheap, efficient creatures along with Æther Vial and some catchall disruption in order to overwhelm its opponents.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Versatile
  • Resilient
  • Very few bad matchups.

Weaknesses

  • The bad matchups are gaining popularity
  • Can sometime get blown out by mass removal.
Infect

Info

Infect is an aggro-combo deck which utilizes the Infect mechanic to win the game quickly. The objective is to give the opponent 10 poison counters. Pump spells are central to the deck, and go hand in hand with Infect creatures.

Signature Cards

Strengths

  • Wins against most combo decks and control decks
  • Fast

Weaknesses

  • Spot removal and blockers
